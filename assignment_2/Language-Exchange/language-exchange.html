Language exchange actively goes by many different terms—

conversation exchange, intercambio, tandem . Regardless of which

term you might be familiar with, they all have the same definition:

“Language exchange is a method of language learning based on mutual

language practicing by learning partners who are speakers of different

languages. This is usually done by two native speakers teaching each

other their native language.” — Wikipedia

In other words, language exchange is the process of joining forces

with someone, known as your language exchange partner , who is

ideally a native speaker of the language you want to learn. Through a

two-way process of direct communication and conversation , both

learners are given the opportunity to improve their target language

skills and proficiency while simultaneously fostering intercultural

competence .

There are two main ways in which language exchange can be carried

out— face-to-face and online:

Face-To-Face Tandem

This is where both partners meet in person and perform a language

exchange. Time should be equally divided between speaking the two

languages so that the learning is fairly distributed. Some may decide to

dedicate the first half of a session to one language before switching to

the other one. Others prefer to speak one language per meeting. The

structure of the language exchange depends on the two participants.

Having this social element means that the participants become fully

immersed in the target language and its culture. This method

particularly focuses on listening comprehension and speaking skills.

Language Exchange Online

Digital communication has made it possible to participate in language

exchange with people all over the world. Also known as etandem, there

are numerous platforms available to facilitate this, from email to

websites and mobile applications. As with face-to-face language

exchange, time should be equally divided between the two languages.

Depending on the medium, this method can improve all four aspects of

language learning— listening, speaking, reading and writing.

Why Language Exchange?

Here are some pros and cons of using language exchange as a

language learning method.

There is no better

way to immerse

yourself in your

target language

than practicing

speaking with a

native speaker.
Improvement in language proficiency:

The most effective way to learn a language is through immersion.

There is no better way to immerse yourself in your target language than

practicing speaking with a native speaker. Language exchange

solidifies what you have learned in the classroom while also offering

real-world exposure to the vernacular language and colloquial

expressions.

It offers a much richer experience of learning a language while also

maintaining a supportive and productive environment where there is

patience and time for understanding.

Development of intercultural competence:

Cultural identity is marked by language, meaning language and culture

are inherently intertwined. Not only is language exchange concerned

with language acquisition, but equally with cultural understanding and

knowledge. Through open communication, you are given an incredible

insight into the customs, traditions, beliefs, and behavior of a society.

There are numerous platforms available to

facilitate language exchange online, from

email to websites and mobile applications

Builds confidence:

Speaking the language you are learning is a daunting challenge for

many. The main reason for this is the fear of making mistakes .

However, mistakes are a natural and integral part of language

acquisition. Having a language exchange forces you to leave your

comfort zone and speak the language, which with time, helps build

your confidence with the language.

Need to have at least a conversational level :

In order to participate in a language exchange, you must be at that

stage of your language learning journey where you can hold a

conversation. Language exchange is, therefore, not a substitute for

formal language education and needs to be used in combination with

other tools and resources.

Finding the right language exchange partner:

To have a successful language exchange, it’s important to find the right

language exchange partner for you. This makes the learning element of

the exchange a lot more fun and a lot less arduous. This may take

some time and effort, however, well worth the investment as you can

not only gain a language exchange partner but a lifelong friend in the

process.
